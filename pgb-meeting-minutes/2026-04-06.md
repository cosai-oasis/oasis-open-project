## Meeting Summary

* **Welcome and Introductions**: The session started with welcoming new members to the PGB: James Norberg, Kyriakos (Rock) Lambros, Zachary Hambrice, Yisroel Mirsky and Kamel Adi.

* **Strategy Paper and Blog Post Publication**: David announced the publication of a Agentic Futures strategy paper along with the accompanying blog post, which are available on the [CoSAI website](https://www.coalitionforsecureai.org/when-the-bots-run-the-incident-response-what-ai-agents-mean-for-enterprise-security/). The paper serves as a follow-up to the strategy paper published in September, given the rapid changes in the field. The intent of the Agentic Futures paper is to orient the community on the direction of agentic futures, using an example from a current CoSAI member to highlight agents collaborating with humans and other agents, and discussing the implications for human oversight.

* **Agentic Identity and Access Control Paper**: The publication of this new paper from WS4 has been approved for release and will be published in an upcoming blog post. Casey confirmed that the blog post is written and is currently going through final checks, with a push to expedite the process for publication potentially this week. 

* **Review of AI Use and AI Governance in CoSAI**: The discussion shifted to reviewing the governance of AI use within CoSAI, specifically regarding the proper attribution of AI-generated content. David noted that there is an open issue in one of the repositories suggesting the use of a generic "co-authored by" tag to maintain community integrity.

* **Policy on External Note-Takers and Agents in Meetings**: A conversation was initiated regarding two new edge cases in AI usage and governance. The first case is the use of non-native note-takers during video conferences, which CoSAI has been denying, preferring to maintain the formal record through CoSAI processes for appropriate sharing and redaction. The second, newer case involves an agent directly joining and submitting content to a mailing list.

  * The direct contribution of an agent to a mailing list, one of CoSAI's two formal channels (the other being GitHub), was flagged as potentially running afoul of the initial governance rules. The original rules mandated that all AI activities into CoSAI engagements must be reviewed and executed by the user in their name. This topic was raised for PGB consideration and feedback, though no immediate resolution was required, with a plan to create a discussion document in the PGB Google Drive to gather opinions.

* **RSAC Working Session Readout**: J.R. provided a readout from the PGB/TSC working session held at the RSA Conference in Cisco's San Francisco offices. The meeting, which had strong attendance, covered the status of Special Interest Groups (SIG1: Security of AI-Assisted Code Development SIG and SIG 2: Security of Agent Development Lifecycle SIG), but the agenda was not fully covered.

* **Discussion of CoSAI Risk Map (CoSAI-RM) and Pilot Use**: A key tangible outcome discussed at the working session was the CoSAI Risk Map (CoSAI-RM), which was noted as progressing well. A suggestion was made to pilot CoSAI-RM within respective enterprises to gather feedback, underscoring the companies' investment in securing AI.

  * The main gap delaying the V1 release of CoSAI-RM is the need to materialize the controls and risks from the workstream papers (Workstream 1, 2, and 4\) into CoSAI-RM. The initial set of personas has been established, and the review process will involve looking at tables summarizing controls, risks, and personas. The preferred approach is to delay the V1 release until the up-to-date content from existing papers is integrated into CoSAI-RM.

* **Clarification of SIG Scope and Contributor Recognition**: Discussions at the working session included clarifying the scope for the new SIGs: Security of AI-assisted Code Development and Security of Agent Development Lifecycle due to a slight overlap, requiring clearer delineation. The discussion also focussed on non-technical topics including the need to recognize contributors, such as workstream and SIG leads, perhaps through awards or press citations, to acknowledge their passionate efforts despite having day jobs.

* **Call for Participation in new SIGs**: A call for participation from member organizations was issued for the new sigs. Security of AI-Assisted Code Development SIG focuses on helping the ecosystem produce secure outputs when using agentic systems and LLMs, which includes Cisco’s Code-Guard donation. The Security of Agent Development Lifecycle SIG focuses on securing the agentic deployment lifecycle by addressing issues like trust boundaries and identity/privilege use. Members and non-members with expertise in these areas were encouraged to participate in the special interest groups to ensure community engagement.

* **Follow-up on Contributor Recognition**: David acknowledged the importance of recognizing member contributions, noting that workstream leads are already invited to the TSC as an internal form of recognition. He proposed taking this topic offline to develop a formal proposal, possibly modeling recognition after initiatives like the OASIS  “new organization of the year” award.

* **Increased Government Engagement and Global Outlook**: David reported increased engagement from government entities at the RSA Conference, who are seeking best practices and standards for securing AI. Specific outreach has been received from Cyber Security Agency of Singapore (CSA), UK Department for Science, Innovation and Technology (DSIT), and the Financial Services Sector Coordinating Council (FSSC), indicating a shift towards a "pull" for insight from CoSAI. The discussion suggested a need for CoSAI to increase global engagement beyond the US, potentially through collaborative roundtables at events like Singapore Cyber Security Week.

* **Global Engagement Strategy**: The need to restart conversations about CoSAI leading global roundtables was discussed, particularly focusing on global engagement with non-policy sides of government entities. The goal is to share practical advice on security challenges and approaches to securing AI systems, with a suggestion to co-sponsor an event with Singapore CSA at Singapore Cyber Week.

* **Impact of Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) on AI Systems**: A new item raised was the need for CoSAI to provide guidance on Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) and its use in agentic and AI systems, which follows Google's commitment to PQC transition by 2029\. This discussion was initiated in Workstream 4, suggesting that a v1.1 of the Model Context Protocol (MCP) paper should highlight the use of PQC algorithms for key exchange in HTTPS transport. The Agentic Identity paper was also noted as an area PQC will impact, but a decision was made to release the first version and follow up with a second version quickly.

* **TSC Updates and Deliverables**: J.R. provided an update on TSC activities, noting a focus on the two new SIGs, the upcoming blog and paper publications for the Agentic Identity paper and that the Shared Responsibility Frameworks, involving AI providers and consumers on compliance requirements, is nearing completion.

* **Workstream 1 Co-Leadership Need**: WS1 is in need of an additional co-lead due to Andre's departure and Matt's upcoming parental leave. WS1 is focused on maturity models around signing and attestation of AI artifacts. The group seeks people deeply engaged in software supply chain security who can work with Jay the remaining lead.

* **New TSC Member and Marketing Update**: Karthik Panda from OpenAI will be joining the TSC, suggesting increased engagement from their organization going forward. Casey reported that the Marketing Subcommittee's primary focus is on the release of the two new blogs. They are exploring ways to increase the cadence of content dissemination, with the podcast idea currently being reconsidered.
