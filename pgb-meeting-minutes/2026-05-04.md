* **Engagement Guidance for Governmental Outreach:**

The objective of this outreach is to achieve brand recognition, influence standards, increase adoption of feedback, and garner better security alignment. Engagement is envisioned as "roundtables" for interactive dialogue, similar to successful engagements at RSAC and other recent events.

He encouraged any members interested in providing feedback or contributing to this global government outreach document should get in touch to be added to the document. He also emphasized that this work is still in progress.  
* **NIST Engagement on AI Risk Management Framework (AIRMF) Profiles:**   
  * The discussion moved to potential engagement with NIST, who have been working on profiles for AIRMF. NIST is focusing on referencing stable guidance and documentation from the ecosystem and has inquired about CoSAI's interest in collaborating. A core question for the PGB and other groups is whether the benefit of engagement outweighs the effort, and what the desired outcomes would be.  
  * The NIST discussion led to a broader conversation about how CoSAI should engage with other standards initiatives, e.g. entities working with ISO on an LLM inference standard. David L. suggested the NIST engagement marks the beginning of a conversation about how CoSAI should participate in these spaces, acknowledging that such efforts might slow down current work. JR, Omar and David L. are scheduled to meet with the NIST representative to gain further insight into their requirements.  
* **Multi-Organization Secure AI Coordination (MOSAIC) gathering in DC:**  
  * Omar provided an update on a meeting held on April 21st, where various organizations, including NIST, the Cloud Security Alliance, OWASP, the Center for Internet Security, the SANS Institute, ITU, the Federal Reserve and CoSAI met in DC. The meeting resulted in a commitment to collaboration rather than having disparate efforts, leading to the potential creation of "MOSAIC" to improve communication and harmonization of standardization efforts.  
  * Rock confirmed the primary goal is agreement on collective work, specifying that the initial focus areas are working with NIST on their taxonomy and potentially an agentic identity standard/taxonomy. Both Omar and Rock agreed that the group intends to follow the CoSAI model for collaboration, primarily using GitHub, and focusing on harmonizing existing outputs rather than creating new forums.  
  * David L. questioned if any minimum deliverables were set to ensure progress, expressing skepticism about the follow-through on such broad ideas. Omar confirmed that establishing a common security taxonomy and glossary, likely hosted by NIST, was recognized as a critical, initial deliverable to ensure all participating organizations are using consistent terminology. Rock confirmed that a GitHub repository is pending creation to facilitate this follow-up.  
* **Strategy for Global Membership Growth and CoSAI Evolution:**   
  * The PGB began a crucial, multi-session discussion on how to evolve CoSAI to maintain its mission of democratized, globally accessible, and recognized security guidance. To enhance the standing of CoSAI's outputs, the group determined a need to grow membership, particularly in regions beyond US-based or US multinational influence. This expansion requires seeking members who bring both global expertise on AI security problems and solutions in regions like EMEA, APAC, India, and Brazil.  
  * Two major challenges to global membership growth were identified: the financial requirement to pay membership dues at the time of joining, which restricts some entities to specific annual budget cycles. The second challenge is the difficulty of engagement for regions APAC with timezone conflicts, requiring CoSAI to consider a "tick-tock cycle" for main meetings (PGB and TSC) to accommodate members in various regions. David L. also asked if CoSAI and OASIS would permit regional hubs or chapters.  
  * David L. proposed seeking approval from OASIS for a "provisional membership" status, allowing entities who missed budget windows to temporarily participate in the PGB until their payment is confirmed by a set deadline. This and other structural changes depend on the PGB's continued alignment that global growth and engagement are critical to CoSAI's mission. Daniel agreed that limiting divergent global regulatory frameworks is an absolute necessity.  
  * David L. reiterated the direct request for all PGB members to use their networks to identify and recruit potential new members who would bring needed regional expertise and standing to CoSAI. In response to the scheduling issue, Daniel proposed moving to a tick-tock cycle for at least one primary meeting front to show commitment to global participation.   
* **CoSAI Contributor Recognition Program Proposal:**   
  * The PGB discussed a proposal drafted by OASIS staff for a CoSAI contributor recognition program, designed to recognize the efforts of workstream participants and members. Daniel strongly supported the concept of individual recognition, especially for key program leads and workstream participants not directly affiliated with a member organization. Kelly proposed opening the recognition document for feedback from the TSC members who initiated the idea.  
* **Refining Governance Language on AI Use for CoSAI:**   
  * The conversation shifted to refining governance language concerning the use of AI tools within CoSAI, particularly for note-taking. The proposed rule states that only the AI note-taking provided by the CoSAI-hosted meeting tool will be used to ensure participants' consent is maintained and community elements are respected. Daniel suggested making the language explicit that the need for official note-taking is to preserve participant consent to the recording, which is currently lost if individuals use their own tools. He also emphasized the need to spell out that an AI-note taker joining a meeting on behalf of a human does not count as attendance.  
  * The group discussed providing clearer language around the use of AI agents for content contribution (e.g., posting to mailing lists or contributing to GitHub). The clarification reinforces that while AI usage is encouraged, the human individual remains fully responsible and accountable for all content provided by the AI system. Daniel suggested the reason for accountability should be stated upfront, and the revised language can be put to a PGB vote via email.  
  * David L. emphasized the need for contributors to tag their submissions when they include elements generated by an AI agent, particularly in GitHub commits, to maintain transparency and awareness. The suggestion is to use a vendor-neutral tag like "Co-authored by: AI Assistant". He committed to surfacing a recommended, pre-vetted tagging language for easier adoption.
