### Summary

* **Welcome and Introductions**  
  * David L. welcomed several new PGB members: Iftach Orr, Anand S, Guy Arazi, Gil Gekker, Mike Caballero.  
* **Call for Consensus: Dedicated Community Engagement Resource Funding**  
  * David L. presented a proposal requesting the allocation of a portion of the CoSAI budget to finance a new part-time position dedicated to community engagement and strategy. This role is envisioned as approximately 20 hours per week and would be filled by an individual with prior experience in managing technical communities. David emphasized that this funding is essential to provide the focused, strategic engagement necessary for CoSAI's growth. The full proposal had been circulated to the PGB two weeks prior to the meeting.  
  * J.R. initiated a discussion regarding the selection and management of the potential hire, specifically inquiring about the proposed review process, the technical qualifications required of the ideal candidate, and the specific performance metrics that would be used to evaluate the success of the role.  
  * David L. clarified that the immediate priority was securing the PGB's approval for the funding commitment itself. Once funding is approved, a smaller, dedicated working group, comprised of himself, J.R. Akila, Omar and OASIS staff, will be tasked with formulating the detailed structural evaluation criteria and concrete success metrics for the role.  
  * Omar expressed strong support for the proposal, underscoring the critical point that this position would be entirely dedicated to CoSAI's mission, separate from general OASIS operations. This structure ensures that CoSAI members will maintain full control over the candidate evaluation, the establishment of the role's strategic roadmap, and its ultimate management.  
  * Daniel stressed that while the new hire will be invaluable, the collective contributions and time investment from existing members remain absolutely necessary for the success of CoSAI. The new role is intended to supplement and enhance, not replace, the efforts of the community.  
  * David called for formal objections to the funding proposal. No objections were raised by any PGB member. Consequently, consensus was officially reached and approved for the proposal to fund the dedicated community engagement resource.  
* **Co-Sponsorship Opportunity: Secure AI Panel in Lille, France**  
  * David L. explained that Google received an invitation from the French government to organize a Secure AI “Secure AI” Roundtable at the upcoming InCyber Forum (FIC) 2026 in Lille on March 31\. Google proposed a formal co-sponsorship with CoSAI, offering CoSAI the opportunity to collaborate on the event's title and bring three CoSAI members to serve as panelists. Critically, this opportunity requires no financial contribution from CoSAI. An email with more information and a call for objections was sent to the PGB mailing list last week.  
  * An objection concerning branding clarity was raised. It was suggested that the event should be clearly branded as either a "Google sponsored event with CoSAI speakers" or a "CoSAI sponsored event with a Google speaker," but not a "co-sponsorship." The concern was centered on avoiding brand dilution and setting a potentially complex precedent for handling future event collaborations with other member firms.  
  * Morgan asked for clarification on the intended focus of the panel content, specifically asking whether the emphasis would be on security best practices or if it would heavily lean toward compliance and data sovereignty issues.  
  * David L. confirmed the primary content intent is Secure AI and AI Red Teaming, though acknowledged that compliance-related topics might naturally arise.  
  * David L. committed to sending one final, formal call for objection via email to the PGB (to be sent by the end of day, New York time).

* **Other Discussion Items**   
  * **AI Attribution in Commits:** A formal proposal was introduced by David L. to mandate explicit disclosure when an AI assistant is used to generate or modify code commits. This would take the form of a vendor-neutral standard (e.g., tagging the commit with specific email). The proposal also includes a requirement to add a statement to repository README files to retroactively cover any previous implicit use of AI assistants in contributions.  
  * **Sharing Agents and Prompts:** David also proposed to standardize the sharing of vendor-neutral definitions of agents and prompts within CoSAI repositories (for example, by creating a dedicated `scripts/agents` directory). The goal is to promote broader collaboration, foster transparency, and allow the community to analyze common patterns in AI usage.  
  * **External Work Highlight:** David L. highlighted several pieces of interesting external work within the broader AI security ecosystem that could potentially inform or influence CoSAI's ongoing work.
