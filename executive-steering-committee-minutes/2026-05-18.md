## Summary

**AI Security Scanning Proposal**: David introduced a Cooperative Security Scanning Framework proposal for scanning of open-source projects. The core problem identified is how to offer security scanning while preventing "distillation," where models learn from or are reverse-engineered from other models during the scanning process. The objective is for CoSAI members to allow cross-model scanning of open-source projects from CoSAI members, using established patterns and exposed endpoints.

* Jason suggested that open source is only one part of the solution space and recommended that the group also consider critical infrastructure, such as wastewater treatment plants, which may lack internal cyber talent. David agreed that the scope could be broadened, but starting with open-source scanning to maintain momentum.

* Jason noted that there is an emerging governmental view that specific capabilities should be restricted to known, trusted parties. This will likely lead to a regulatory framework that changes how the proposal is implemented, as members would need to meet certain requirements to participate. David acknowledged this and stated they should incorporate these potential requirements into their planning.

* J.R. asked for clarification on how this relates to existing efforts regarding Mythos and tailored clustered access for cyber. David clarified that the objective is to allow members to compete in the market while ensuring the ecosystem remains secure and that they do not lose control of their models. The long-term goal is to allow secure scanning of proprietary codebases without risking data leaks, thereby benefiting the entire ecosystem.

* David clarified that there is no mandate to use any specific endpoint, and any member—including those outside the U.S.—can participate in the scanning ecosystem at their own discretion. Omar expressed concern regarding the complexity of implementation across an international ecosystem.

* David explained that the implementation would rely on technical gating, rate limiting, and confidential containers to prevent distillation. The system would also manage chargebacks and capacity constraints. The proposal does not force members to interact with models they do not trust, nor does it override existing legal or domain-based restrictions.

**Governmental Outreach and Strategic Communications**: David presented the CoSAI Governmental Outreach Strategy document emphasizing the need to define the organization's goals and opportunities. The strategy should consider event logistics, and the group needs to act quickly to secure slots in upcoming forums, which often require months of lead time.

* Omar proposed that CoSAI should host its own event(s) in Washington, D.C. to bring people to the CoSAI table rather than CoSAI looking for a table to join. Jason agreed, suggesting that this is a policy proposal rather than a technical one, and could be moved forward by the PGB. David supported this, emphasizing the need to host something by July.

* J.R. suggested targeting specific entities like NIST or participating in international forums such as the ITU in Geneva. David agreed, noting that he was invited to the ITU SG17 plenary session in June. He expressed willingness to attend if the group believes there is value in making connections there for CoSAI.

* J.R. emphasized the importance of measuring outcomes when engaging with external forums like the ITU. David agreed, noting that the group needs to decide if they truly want to focus on this problem domain, as that decision will dictate how they structure their engagement, messaging, and future events.

* Omar highlighted the urgency of these actions because other groups,are beginning to organize around similar topics. He recommended that CoSAI should contribute from the start and share evidence of how they use models such as CoSAI-RM internally. David reiterated that they need to finalize their direction as a group.

The PGB would approve these buckets, and execution would occur through the appropriate working groups to avoid voting fatigue. The proposed buckets could include events, content creation, website updates, and strategic staffing.

* Omar inquired about the status of hiring a dedicated, part-time outreach staff to support initiatives. David suggested that hiring should be integrated into the budget "buckets" approach once there is agreement on priorities and committed to meeting with Omar to flush out the outreach role.
