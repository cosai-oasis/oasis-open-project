## Meeting Summary – Based on AI-generated summary

**TSC Governance Change**: The participants reviewed the need to expand the Technical Steering Committee (TSC) co-chairs beyond two members, aiming for a minimum of three to manage increased workloads and facilitate better regional representation. There was no opposition to this proposal, and David requested that the OASIS staff draft the necessary language to support this change for a vote during the next PGB meeting.

**OASIS Staffing Update**: David informed the group that Kelly is no longer with the OASIS team and that the organization is actively managing the transition to ensure continued, appropriate staffing coverage.

**Dues Pooling**: The group reviewed the proposal to pool premier member dues. David confirmed that if a majority of premier members agree, the funds will be pooled, and OASIS staff were tasked with circulating the necessary documentation to all premier members for their approval, requesting David and Omar be copied on the correspondence.

**CoSAI Budget Proposal**: David presented the CoSAI budget proposal, clarifying that the document serves to authorize the use of dues across various entities rather than providing fine-grained, line-item details. He emphasized that the group is not voting on individual activities at this stage, but rather on the strategic breakdown and allocation of funds, which will remain flexible as government outreach plans develop.

* Jason advocated for focusing on earned media rather than paid media, expressing a desire for CoSAI to contribute to significant, world-changing events and conversations. The participants agreed to restructure the budget proposal to focus on percentage allocations and desired outcomes, such as ecosystem engagement, rather than specific activities, to better align with these strategic goals.

* J.R. suggested that budgets should aspirationally link specific investments to desired outcomes, such as engagement metrics or downloads. David and Omar committed to integrating this outcome-focused narrative into the budget proposal over the next two weeks to ensure the document reflects how resources directly enable specific, measurable goals.

**Government Outreach**: Omar highlighted the importance of using CoSAI's outreach efforts to provide high-level education regarding sovereign AI and other government-related engagements. The participants agreed that clarifying these high-level objectives would simplify the approval process and help move initiatives forward.

* David confirmed plans to update the outreach document to include Chatham House rules, address the securing of advanced AI usage, and resolve versioning issues. The group discussed balancing near-term engagements over the summer with longer-term goals like Singapore International Cyber Week, with David seeking input on how to structure these efforts for the next PGB meeting.

**Recognition Program**: The participants discussed the status of the draft recognition program, with Omar noting that it represents a low-risk, positive initiative for community motivation. There was consensus to move forward with a clean version of the document for a call for objections before the next PGB meeting.

**AI Usage Guidelines**: David proposed finalizing the AI usage guidelines, which include provisions for "virtual employees" and agentic content. While discussing technical constraints—specifically regarding GitHub commit language— participants agreed to prioritize simplicity and use "assisted by" language over perfect technical implementation, deciding to move the document to a vote .

**Identity Delegation in Agentic AI**: Daniel introduced a potential new area for CoSAI involvement regarding identity delegation and revocation patterns in Agentic AI. David and other members discussed the strategic value of bringing such standards to CoSAI, noting the benefit of existing synergies within OASIS and the desire to encourage broader participation.

* The group debated how to establish guiding principles for where CoSAI should engage, with Sunil suggesting the documentation of principles focused on preventing consumer harm. Participants agreed to use these discussions, including the topics of identity and cooperative scanning, to help shape the scope of CoSAI's future efforts.

* David presented a proposal regarding cooperative security scanning and trusted inference, framing it as a way to assist the ecosystem with establishing security boundaries. The participants continued to deliberate on whether this fits within the scope of CoSAI, with a consensus on the need to maintain an open dialogue about which security-focused AI challenges the group will actively address.

* Jason proposed sharing observations on how Frontier AI labs and other organizations utilize AI within their Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC). The group expressed interest in this idea, suggesting that members could share memos or vignettes to provide a unified CoSAI perspective on how AI impacts development, while emphasizing that traditional tools remain relevant alongside AI tools.

* Jason proposed a simple, lightweight strategy for publishing a blog post and an accompanying LinkedIn post. The proposed content would feature three to four paragraphs summarizing observations and patterns, provided the team secures at least three vignettes from three distinct companies. Jason volunteered to write the initial draft with an impetus for others who would like to share their views.
